How to Calculate Zakat

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam. It is an annual obligation to give 2.5% of your net wealth to those in need, provided your wealth exceeds the nisab threshold.

The nisab is the minimum amount of wealth that makes Zakat obligatory. It is equivalent to 85 grams of gold or 595 grams of silver. Most scholars recommend using the gold nisab. Your wealth must have been held for one full lunar year (hawl).

Add up all zakatable assets (cash, gold, silver, investments, business inventory), subtract short-term debts, and if the total exceeds nisab, pay 2.5% of the total.
