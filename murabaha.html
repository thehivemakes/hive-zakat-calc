How Murabaha Financing Works

Murabaha is a cost-plus financing arrangement permitted under Islamic law. The financier purchases the asset and sells it to the buyer at the original cost plus an agreed profit margin, payable in installments.

Unlike conventional loans, the profit is fixed at the time of the contract. There is no compounding interest. The total amount owed never increases, even if payments are late (though late fees may apply as a penalty donated to charity).
