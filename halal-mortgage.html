Comparing Conventional and Islamic Home Financing

Islamic home financing avoids interest (riba) by structuring the transaction as a sale (Murabaha) or lease-to-own (Ijara). The total cost may be similar to conventional mortgages, but the structure is fundamentally different.

In Murabaha home financing, the bank buys the property and sells it to you at cost plus profit, payable in installments. The total price is fixed and does not compound. In Ijara, you lease the property with an option to purchase at the end.
